Kadhi pakora

Description

Kadhi Pakora is a traditional North Indian dish made with a tangy yogurt-based curry thickened with gram flour and flavored with spices. Crispy fritters (pakoras) are added to the kadhi, making it hearty and comforting. It is often served with steamed rice or roti and is a staple in many households.

Ingredients:

For the paakoras(FRITTERS)

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 finely chopped green chili
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Oil for deep frying

For the kadhi (YOGURT-BASED CURRY)

  • 2 cups yogurt (curd)
  • 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For the tadka(TEMPERING)

  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the pakoras (fritters):
    • In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, chopped green chili, red chili powder, turmeric powder, carom seeds, and salt.
    • Add water gradually to form a thick batter.
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
    • Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil and fry until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the kadhi (yogurt-based curry):
    • In a large bowl, whisk together yogurt and gram flour until smooth. Gradually add water to make a thin batter.
    • Heat oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dried red chilies, and asafoetida. Sauté until the seeds start to splutter.
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir well.
    • Slowly pour in the yogurt mixture while continuously stirring to prevent lumps.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the pakoras:
    • Once the kadhi has thickened slightly, add the fried pakoras to the kadhi. Let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes so that the pakoras absorb some of the flavors.
  4. Prepare the tadka (tempering):
    • In a small pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and asafoetida. Sauté until the seeds splutter.
    • Pour this tadka over the kadhi for added flavor.
  5. Garnish and serve:
    • Garnish the kadhi pakora with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
